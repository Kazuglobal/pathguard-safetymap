## Summary

This document defines the analysis frame for a simulated UX research exercise covering 100 virtual target users.

## Product Assumptions

- The app is primarily for parents of elementary school children.
- The core value is helping parents confirm the safety of their child's school route.
- Based on repository context, the app likely includes route registration, route viewing on a map, and location-based safety context.
- Because the user-provided app description was left blank, all feature-level conclusions are scenario-based rather than validated by direct product copy.

## Research Goal

Produce a structured UX research-style synthesis that approximates what 100 target users would think, focusing on:

- first impressions
- top usability issues
- missing features
- reasons for churn and retention
- product improvement priorities

## Virtual Sample Frame

The 100 simulated users are distributed across these dimensions:

- safety anxiety: low / medium / high
- time pressure: low / high
- digital confidence: low / medium / high
- child grade: lower elementary / upper elementary
- commuting context: walking alone / walking with friends / parent accompaniment

## Analysis Method

The synthesis will combine:

- attitude segmentation by parent type
- first-use and repeated-use journey thinking
- prioritization based on frequency, severity, and trust impact

## Output Rules

- Follow the user's requested Japanese output structure.
- Rank findings in practical order rather than exhaustive order.
- Treat all findings as simulated research insights, not observed field data.
